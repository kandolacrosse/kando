Learn Lacrosse (Properly)

For people who didn’t grow up playing. No American bias. No elite waffle. Just what actually helps at uni.

  • Attend an intro session
  • Observe the team culture
  • Ask questions freely

Even if you’ve played rugby or football before, lacrosse demands a different level of mobility and athleticism. Focus on stamina, VO₂ max, hip flexibility, and wrist strength. Starting early makes sessions easier and more fun.

  • Borrow a stick if possible
  • Get the essential helmet/gloves
  • Wear trainers you can run in comfortably

Gear doesn’t need to be top-end. Comfort and safety > flashiness. Borrow from club if needed.

  • Start with wallballing religiously
  • Focus on catching & passing
  • Basic cradling and short-range shooting

Wallballing is the foundation. I spent hours at home hitting the wall, and every skill felt natural after that. Treat it like a daily ritual.

  • Watch the flow of the game
  • Learn basic positions & responsibilities
  • Ask about formations

Observing scrimmages and asking questions makes joining drills far less overwhelming.

  • Chat with teammates off the pitch
  • Attend club socials & group chats
  • Build confidence through connections

Team environment is as important as sport itself. Comfort socially = better on-field performance.
